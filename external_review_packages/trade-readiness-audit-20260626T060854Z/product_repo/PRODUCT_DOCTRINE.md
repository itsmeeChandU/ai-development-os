# Product Doctrine

Importer Source Readiness Copilot organizes source-readiness evidence. It helps
customers and operators see what is known, what is missing, which claims are
blocked, and what review is required before action.

## What The Product Does

- collects source packet fields
- organizes evidence and official references
- creates conservative blockers
- shows next valid moves
- exports safe readiness reports
- routes high-risk packets toward operator or expert review
- keeps an audit-friendly boundary around every claim

## What The Product Does Not Do

- approve imports
- confirm tariffs
- certify CFIA compliance
- provide customs, tariff, legal, financial, or compliance advice
- recommend suppliers
- validate buyers
- replace licensed brokers, lawyers, accountants, or qualified reviewers
- guarantee public launch or production readiness

## Core Rule

No evidence, no claim.

Stale evidence blocks the claim.

Reference-only evidence supports reference-only language.

AI-simulated review can create blockers and next valid moves. It cannot open
human approval gates.

Human or expert review only applies to the exact scope reviewed.

## Current Status Language

Use:

```text
Internal logic ready - external claims blocked
```

Do not use customer-facing labels such as:

```text
approved
compliant
safe_to_import
tariff_confirmed
CFIA_cleared
supplier_verified
buyer_validated
```

unless real scoped evidence supports that exact claim.
